Champunga mkati Recipe

br>

is one of the most common Malawian snacks that is likely to be found in most market places. This snack is not only nutritious, it is also delicious and easy to make. It combines the creaminess of rice with a crispy, golden exterior.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • salt
  • All purpose Wheat flour:
  • Cooking oil
  • Yeast
  • Onions:
  • Food colourant
  • Salt:
  • Water:
  • Food colourant:

Steps

  1. Step 1: Boil water and add 1 kg of rice and salt to taste. Stir gently and leave it to cook.
  2. Step 2: Leave the rice to cook for 15 to 20 minutes whilst stirring occasionally.
  3. Step 3: Take the rice off fire, drain excess water and leave it to cool.
  4. Step 4: In a bowl, pour 300 Mls of warm water, then add yeast, food colourant and salt to taste. Stir the mixture and add flour whilst stirring. Keep adding flour until the dough reaches the right consistency
  5. Step 5: Leave the dough to rise for 30-45 minutes.
  6. Step 6: Heat cooking oil in a frying pot or pan.
  7. Step 7: Chop onions in small pieces and mix it well with the rice.
  8. Step 8: Shape the rice mixture into balls or desired shapes.
  9. Step 9: Cover the rice with the prepared dough.
  10. Step 10: Fry the rice balls until crispy outside.
  11. Step 11: Our Champunga Mkati is ready to serve. It can be served with a salad of your choice, a sauce and any beverage.
